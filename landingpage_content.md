## **How Ouroboros works**  See the whole kill chain, break it automatically.

Step 1 — Discover your living attack surface  
Ouroboros continuously ingests from Git, CI/CD, cloud providers, K8s, service meshes, WAFs, and identity systems. It builds a live graph of assets, services, users, data stores, and their relationships — across environments.

Step 2 — Think like an attacker, at scale  
The RED side runs continuous, autonomous campaigns against that graph: chaining misconfigurations, vulnerable code, exposed services, weak identities, and supply chain issues into real attack paths that end at your critical data and systems.

Step 3 — Generate fixes, not wishlists  
The BLUE side designs changes across layers — code patches, infra-as-code diffs, policy updates, network rules, and identity tightening. Fix plans are aware of blast radius, SLOs, and change windows, so they align with how your org actually ships.

Step 4 — Test in twins, then in the wild  
Every fix is exercised in a high‑fidelity “digital twin” environment, then rolled out with canaries and feature flags in production. Ouroboros watches real metrics and live traffic; if anything smells wrong, it rolls back faster than a human Slack thread can start.

Step 5 — Re‑attack and close the loop  
After deployment, RED re‑runs the full attack path against production to confirm the chain is actually broken. Only then is a risk marked as “resolved,” and the exposure graph updated.

Step 6 — Explain it so humans can sleep  
For each attack path, Ouroboros leaves behind a paper trail: what the path was, how it worked, what changed, who approved, when it was deployed, how it behaved in prod, and why it’s no longer exploitable — mapped to the controls and regulations you care about.
